Philanthropy

Taylor Swift is well known for her philanthropic deeds

Swift has been given a number of awards because of her philanthropy, including being ranked at number 1 on DoSomething's "Gone Good" list, the 'star of compassion' from the Tennessee Disaster Services, amongst other things.

She has donated to a number of relief funds over the past few years for natural disasters, using her large amount of wealth for good. In 2008 she donated $100,000 to the Red Cross to help victims of the Iowa Flood. She has also performed at a number of charity events, including Sydney's Sound Relief concert and using her own concerts to raise money for victims of other natural disasters. In 2016, she donated $1 million to the Louisiana Flood Relief efforts and $100,000 to the Dolly Parton Fire Fund.

Swift is also a supporter of the arts

Swift has done quite a bit for Nashville, TN when it comes to the arts, being a benefactor of the Nashville Songwriters Hall of Fame. She has also donated $75000 to Nashville's Hendersonville High School to help refurbish the school auditorium, $4 million to fund the building of a new education center at the Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum in Nashville, $60000 to the music departments of 6 U.S. universities, and $100000 to Nashville Symphony, an American sympony orchestra based in Nashville.

Swift has also donated book and money to various schools around the country in effort to improve education, and donated items to multiple charities for auction, including UNICEF Tap Project and MusiCares.

Swift has also done her part helping children's hospitals, donating $25000 to St. Jude Children's Research Hospital in 2011 and $50000 to the Children's Hospital of Philidelphia. In 2012 she participated in the Stand Up to Cancer telethon, performing her charity single titled "Ronan", written in memory of a 4-year-old boy who died of neuroblastoma.

Swift has worked for and with other artists as well

Swift donated to fellow singer-songwriter Kesha to help with her legal battles against Dr.Luke, and donated to Mariska Hargitay's Joyful Heart Foundation organization. Swift also performed "Soon You'll Get Better" during a benefit concert curated by Lady Gaga for Global Citizen to raise money for the World Health Organization's COVID-19 Solidarity Response Fund. In 2018 and 2021, Swift donated to the Rape, Abuse, and Incest National Network in honor of Sexual Assault Awareness and Prevention Month. In addition to these charitable donations, Swift has also donated to her own fans several times for their medical or academic expenses.
