Trivia and Fun Facts about Taylor Swift

Swift Could Become a Billionaire From U.S Tour Dates Alone

Swift's current net worth is estimated at $570 million according to Forbes, and her upcomming 'Eras' Tour is set to bring in almost that amount in revenue. David Herlihy, a Northeastern University Teaching Professor estimated that Swift with earn around $620 million from the tour, while Billboard estimates that 'Eras' will generate $591 million. The average price for tickets to the tour is $215, and there is more than 2 million tickets to be sold, along with the revenue made off of merchandise sold at each concert. This amount of money will also bring Swift to the top of the list of top-grossing female touring artists, knocking down Madonna's Sticky & Sweet tour of 2009 which held the previous record of $407 million in sales.

Swift Caused a Ticket Master Crash

Speaking of the 'Eras' tour, when Swift first announced the tour and presale began, Ticketmaster, one of the biggest ticket selling websites, crashed and many fans were left not knowing if they would get a ticket or not. The company eventually cancelled the sale due to the high demand and not being able to handle it, Swift later taking to twitter, protective of her fanbase and eager to get the situation figured out.

Swift's Private Jet Emissions Controversy

A U.K. based sustainability marketing firm called Yard shared a report in July of 2022 revealing Swift's private jet flew 170 times between Jan 1 and July 19, the total airtime being around 16 days in the air. The total emissions from these flights totaled to 8,293.54 tonnes of carbon. This is 1,184.8 times more than the average person's total annual emissions. A spokeperson for Swift stated that the jet was regularly loaned to other individuals, so it wasn't correct for all the blame to fall on the popstar, but she did get a lot of backlash for this observation.

Multiple Lawsuits

Swift has been through a couple lawsuits of people claiming she'd stolen lines or ideas from them. Swift's 2013 single "Shake it Off" was the subject of a lawsuit by songwriters who penned 3LW's "Playas Gon' Play", which was released in 2001 and had the lyrics "playas, they gonna play" and "haters, they gonna hate". Despite Swift's attemtps to dismiss the case, after the copyright claims were filed in 2017 a federal judge ruled that Swift would have to face a jury trial.

More recently, a woman named Teresa La Dart sued Swift for more than $1 million, claiming she copied her 2010 poetry book under the name "Lover" when Swift made a booklet to acompany her album, also named "Lover." La Dart claimed Swift's team used her book as inspiration for the color scheme, photography style and general content of the booklet.
